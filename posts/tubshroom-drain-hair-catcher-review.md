I have long hair. I live with someone who also has long hair. Our shower drain used to clog every six weeks like clockwork. We tried mesh covers — hair went around them. We tried Drano — it worked once, then stopped. We considered shaving our heads.

Then we bought a TubShroom. It's been eight months and the drain has not clogged once.

![A close-up of a shower drain with a small mushroom-shaped hair catcher inserted into it](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1583847268964-b28dc8f51f92?w=1200&q=80&auto=format&fit=crop)

## How it works

It's a silicone tube the shape of a small mushroom. The stem sits inside the drain opening; the cap rests flush with the floor of the tub. Water flows around the stem freely. Hair, however, wraps around it.

You don't see the hair. You just see the small silicone cap. Once a week (less if you have short hair), you pull it out, slide off the wrapped hair with a piece of toilet paper, and put it back. The whole job takes thirty seconds.

## Why this one

- Fits the standard 1.4–2.0 inch tub drain (measure first!)
- Sits flush — looks almost invisible
- Captures essentially 100% of hair
- Silicone won't rust, crack, or mold
- Costs less than one plumber visit, lasts years

## The honest cons

I want to be specific about these, because every glowing review I read missed them, and they'll matter to some buyers:

**The weekly cleaning is genuinely gross.** What you pull out looks like a small wet rat. The first time I cleaned it I almost gagged. After a month, you stop noticing. After three months, you take a quiet pride in how full it gets — proof that it's all hair that *isn't* in your pipes.

**If you forget to clean it for two weeks, drainage slows.** This is by far the most common one-star complaint, and it's not a product flaw — it's a maintenance flaw. The TubShroom works exactly because it catches hair. Hair piles up. Water gets through more slowly. The fix is a 30-second clean. People who leave one-star reviews didn't do the clean.

**It only fits standard drains.** Measure your drain opening before buying. The DrainWig, ShowerShroom, and SinkShroom variants exist for other configurations. Buying the wrong one is the second most common complaint.

> The TubShroom is a deal where you trade a tiny weekly chore for the disappearance of an enormous occasional disaster. That trade is, in my view, one of the best in modern domestic life.

## Who shouldn't buy it

If you live alone, have short hair, and have never clogged a drain — you don't need this. If you're squeamish about cleaning and your partner refuses to do it either, you'll end up with a slow drain and an unhappy review. Be honest about whose job it'll be.

For everyone else with long hair: it's $14. You will spend that much on a single bottle of drain cleaner in three months. This is the better trade.

I keep one in the master bath and one in the guest bath. The third one is in a drawer, waiting. That's the highest compliment I can pay a product.
