I bought my first monitor stand in 2019. It was made from particleboard with a glossy black laminate, wobbled when I typed, and started peeling at the corners within a year. I've been quietly testing alternatives ever since.

This one — a single slab of solid bamboo, sanded smooth — has been on my desk for eight months. It is the first one I haven't wanted to replace.

![A bamboo monitor stand on a wooden desk with a 27-inch monitor on top](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1547119957-637f8679db1e?w=1200&q=80&auto=format&fit=crop)

## What it actually does

It lifts your monitor 4.7 inches. That number sounds arbitrary until you sit at your desk and realize your eyes naturally rest about four inches above where the top of your screen used to be. The headache I'd been blaming on hydration was, it turns out, my neck tilting downward for nine hours a day.

The bamboo is dense — not the airy, splintery kind you find on cheap cutting boards. It feels closer to a hardwood butcher's block. There's no veneer to peel, no glue smell, no surprise screws rattling inside.

## Why this one

- Solid bamboo, sanded smooth — no veneer, no glue smell
- Lifts a 27-inch monitor to perfect eye height (4.7 inches)
- Slides a low-profile keyboard underneath when not in use
- Holds up to 44 lbs without flex — tested with a 32-inch ultrawide
- Arrives in one piece, no assembly, no tools required

## The small details that matter

The corners are gently rounded, so when you rest your wrist while reaching for something, it doesn't dig in. The underside has felt pads — not the rubber dots that yellow and crumble in a year, but actual felt. Three of them. Spaced so the stand doesn't rock on uneven surfaces.

> The best products are the ones you stop noticing. After a week, this stand felt like part of the desk. That's the highest compliment I can give it.

I keep a small notebook and a pen under it now. The space underneath is exactly the height of a Moleskine. I don't think this was intentional, but I appreciate it anyway.

## A small note on price

It's $58. There are $20 versions on Amazon — I tried two of them. Both arrived warped. The math worked out: I spent $40 on returns and replacements before giving up.

If you've been putting your monitor on a stack of textbooks because the alternatives all look like office furniture from 2003, this is the one to stop the search.
